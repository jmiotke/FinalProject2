Teapot Overview

Mineral Clay Teapots

Many teapots are made of mineral clay. There are commonly five colors of mineral clay teapots: purple, red, yellow, green, and black. Yellow and green are the rarest colors. Purple and red are the most common. Mineral tea pots are very popular in China because the clay lowers the pH, or acidity, of the tea brewed inside the clay pot. The porous nature of the clay tea pots also make the tea taste more fresh.

Mineral clay teapots can be very expensive, typically depending upon the experience and skill of the artist who made the pot. Balanance and roundness are important features to clay teapots. Artists will typically inscribe their names on their teapots.

Porcelain Teapots

Porcelain teapots have a glaze that seals off the pot. Unlike the mineral clay pots, the tea is unable to seep into the teapot walls. Green tea is commonly brewed in porcelain teapots.
