Oolong Tea

Overview

Oolong tea is grown in the southern portion of China (e.g., south of the Yellow River), primarily in the Guangdong provice of China and Taiwan. These areas have the correct temperatures and humidity to grow the Oolong tea bushes that produce Oolong tea. The adult leaves are picked for Oolong tea, which are typically harvested in May. Oolong tea is partially fermented, falling between black tea (completely fermented) and green tea (not fermented at all).

Oolong is typically regarded as a "detoxification" tea, and has been know to have weight loss properties. In addition, oolong tea has been used to lower the effects of alcohol consumption.

Preparation

The oolong tea is shaved from the cake, and then placed in the strainer over the teapot. The first water poured over the oolong tea is not consumed because this first water removes debris and "wakes up" the tea.

Bring spring or freshly drawn filtered water to 190℉. Place the oolong leaves directly into your teapot. Fill the pot with hot water, allowing the leaves to sit for no more than 5 seconds, then discard the water. (Source: https://www.smithtea.com › pages › how-to-brew-oolong-teas)
