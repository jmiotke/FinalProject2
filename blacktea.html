Black Tea

Overview

Black tea is grown throughout China. Black tea is completely fermented and typically has a stronger flavor when compared with other teas. While some teas lose their flavor within a year, black tea can retain its flavor for years. This longevity made black tea popular throughout the world.

Preparation

Generally, 4 grams (0.14 oz) of tea per 200 ml (7.0 imp fl oz; 6.8 US fl oz) of water.[14] Unlike green teas, which turn bitter when brewed at higher temperatures, black tea should be steeped in water brought up to 90–95 °C. The first brew should be 60 sec., the second brew 40 sec., and the third brew 60 sec. If the tea is of high quality, it may be brewed several times by progressively adding 10 sec. to the brew time following the third infusion (note: when using a larger tea pot the ratio of tea to water will need to be adjusted to achieve similar results). (Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_tea)
