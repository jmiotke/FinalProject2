Pu-Ehr Tea

Overview

Pu-Ehr tea is grown in Fujian province, located in southwestern China. It is typically produced into a brick-like shape. Pu-Ehr tea comes from a tree rather than bushes. Pu-Ehr tea relies upon fermentation using enzymes, and for this reason it is packed in paper rather than than other air-tight materials. It is typically packed in round cakes. The tea is then removed from the cake layer by layer, rather than removing an entire piece (e.g., like cutting and removing a piece of pie). Pu-Ehr tea is thought to reduce blood pressure and blood sugar levels. A person should drink Pu-Ehr tea for at least three to five months before expecting to experience these effects.

Preparation

Bring fresh, filtered water to a boil (212℉). Optionally, preheat your cup with boiling water, then discard the water. After measuring the desired amount of tea leaves, pour the leaves in the teapot and pour 8-10 ounces of water directly over the leaves. Allow the leaves to steep for 5 minutes.
